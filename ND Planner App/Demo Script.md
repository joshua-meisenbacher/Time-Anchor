# Time Anchor Demo Script

## Goal

Show that Time Anchor is not just a planner. It adapts to different users and different kinds of days so the plan feels more workable, more humane, and easier to follow.

## Demo Length

60 to 90 seconds

## Recording Setup

- Start on the `Settings` tab.
- Open `Demo Stories`.
- Record in the iPhone simulator with light mode unless you want the lower-stimulation story to stand out more in dark mode.
- Use the `Load + Check-In` and `Load + Today` buttons so the transitions feel intentional and fast.

## Script

### Opening

Voiceover:

"Time Anchor is a planner designed around how people actually move through a day, especially when focus, transitions, and energy are inconsistent. Instead of showing one generic schedule, this demo shows how the app adapts to different users and different situations."

On screen:

- Open `Settings`
- Tap `Demo Stories`
- Pause briefly on the three story cards

### Story 1: Jordan

Voiceover:

"Jordan is a student balancing class and an afternoon work shift. The challenge is not just having a lot to do. It is losing the best study window to anxiety and context switching. Time Anchor helps by protecting the morning study block, making the work transition explicit, and reducing the pressure to keep doing school tasks right before work."

On screen:

- Tap `Load + Check-In` for `Student Between Class and Work`
- Pause on the `Check-In` screen
- Tap `Today`
- Highlight the study block and work transition anchors

### Story 2: Sara

Voiceover:

"Sara is coordinating family responsibilities while carrying a crowded, high-pressure day. Here the app helps by turning overload into visible anchors, surfacing the heaviest commitment first, and keeping recovery and shutdown visible before the day spills into tomorrow."

On screen:

- Return to `Settings > Demo Stories`
- Tap `Load + Today` for `Family Coordinator in Overload`
- Pause on the morning triage and client deliverable areas
- Briefly scroll to the evening shutdown anchor

### Story 3: Alex

Voiceover:

"Alex is having a recovery day. This is important because support should not always mean pushing for more output. Time Anchor can also reduce demands, protect recovery time, and keep just one practical task visible so the day still feels held together."

On screen:

- Return to `Settings > Demo Stories`
- Tap `Load + Today` for `Recovery Without Losing the Thread`
- Pause on the recovery anchors
- Let the lower-stimulation visual style sit on screen for a beat

### Closing

Voiceover:

"The value of Time Anchor is not just organizing tasks. It is helping different users move through real days with less friction, less overload, and more support that actually fits."

On screen:

- Return to the `Demo Stories` list or stay on Alex's Today view
- Hold the final frame for one second before ending

## 30-Second Version

Voiceover:

"Time Anchor is a planner that adapts to the user, not the other way around. In this demo, Jordan gets help protecting a study block before work, Sara gets support triaging an overloaded day, and Alex gets a plan built around recovery instead of pressure. The app turns different kinds of days into support that feels more realistic, more humane, and easier to follow."

## Optional Caption

"Time Anchor helps users navigate focus, transitions, overload, and recovery with plans that adapt to the day they are actually having."
