# Time Anchor Mentor Video Demo

## Goal

Create a polished 75 to 90 second demo that explains the product clearly enough that your mentor can understand the concept just by watching.

## Tone

Calm, confident, and product-focused.

This should feel less like "here are my screens" and more like "here is the user problem, here is the product idea, and here is how the app responds."

## Video Structure

1. What Time Anchor is
2. Why it matters
3. Three fast user examples
4. What makes the product different

## Final Script

### Opening

Voiceover:

"This is Time Anchor, a planner designed around the idea that support should adapt to the user and to the kind of day they are actually having."

"Instead of treating every day like a generic productivity problem, the app helps users navigate focus, transitions, overload, and recovery in a more realistic way."

On screen:

- Start on `Settings`
- Open `Demo Stories`
- Let the three story cards appear clearly

### Story 1

Voiceover:

"The first example is Jordan, a student balancing class and an afternoon work shift. The challenge here is not just having too much to do. It is losing the best study window to anxiety and context switching."

"Time Anchor protects that morning work block, makes the shift to work explicit, and reduces the pressure to keep doing school tasks right before the job starts."

On screen:

- Tap `Load + Check-In` for `Student Between Class and Work`
- Pause on the check-in state
- Move to `Today`
- Briefly highlight the study block and work transition anchors

### Story 2

Voiceover:

"The second example is Sara, who is carrying an overloaded day with coordination responsibilities and too many competing demands."

"Here the app helps by turning the day into clearer anchors, surfacing the heaviest commitment first, and keeping recovery and shutdown visible so the day does not keep expanding."

On screen:

- Return to `Demo Stories`
- Tap `Load + Today` for `Family Coordinator in Overload`
- Pause on the morning triage and primary work block
- Scroll just enough to show the recovery or shutdown portion

### Story 3

Voiceover:

"The third example is Alex on a recovery day. This matters because support should not always mean pushing for more output."

"In this case, the app lowers pressure, protects restorative time, and still keeps one practical task visible so the day feels supported without becoming demanding."

On screen:

- Return to `Demo Stories`
- Tap `Load + Today` for `Recovery Without Losing the Thread`
- Hold on the lower-stimulation interface and recovery anchors

### Closing

Voiceover:

"What I’m exploring with Time Anchor is a planning experience that adjusts to real human variability."

"The product value is not just organizing tasks. It is helping different users move through different kinds of days with less friction and more fitting support."

On screen:

- End on Alex's screen or return to the `Demo Stories` overview
- Hold the final frame for one second

## Recording Directions

- Keep the total runtime under 90 seconds.
- Use a steady speaking pace with short pauses after each story.
- Avoid tapping too quickly. Let each screen sit long enough to read.
- If possible, trim out any dead time between loading states.
- Do one clean take for audio, then match the screen recording to it.

## What To Show

- `Settings > Demo Stories`
- Jordan with `Load + Check-In`
- Sara with `Load + Today`
- Alex with `Load + Today`

That is enough for the first polished mentor demo. Do not try to show every tab.
