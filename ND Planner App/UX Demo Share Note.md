Hi! I put together a demo concept for my app, Time Anchor, and I’d love your UX feedback.

Time Anchor is a planner designed around the idea that planning support should adapt to the user and to the kind of day they are actually having. Instead of treating every day like a productivity problem, the app tries to support focus, transitions, overload, and recovery in a more human way.

For the demo, I structured it around three user scenarios rather than a feature tour:

1. Jordan, a student balancing class and an afternoon work shift.
The app protects a study block, makes the transition to work more explicit, and tries to reduce context switching before the shift.

2. Sara, a family coordinator moving through an overloaded day.
The app turns a crowded day into clearer anchors, surfaces the heaviest commitment first, and keeps recovery and shutdown visible before burnout spills into tomorrow.

3. Alex, a user on a recovery day.
The app lowers pressure, protects restorative time, and still keeps one practical task visible so the day feels held together without demanding too much.

What I’m trying to communicate in the demo is that the value is not just task organization. It’s that the app changes its support depending on who the user is and what kind of day they’re having.

The main UX questions I’d love feedback on are:

1. Does this user-centered framing make the concept feel clearer and more compelling than a traditional feature walkthrough?
2. Do these three scenarios feel distinct enough, or do they blur together?
3. If you were seeing this for the first time, what feels strongest: the problem framing, the adaptive support idea, or the interface itself?
4. What feels missing if I wanted this to land as a strong portfolio case study or product demo?

I also put together a short 60 to 90 second script version for recording, but I’m especially interested in whether the demo narrative itself makes sense before I refine visuals further.

If anything feels confusing, too broad, or not yet convincing, that kind of feedback would be especially helpful.
